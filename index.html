Common surface condition

Does your concrete look tired?

Cracks, scaling and discoloration do not always mean the entire slab must be replaced.

Some concrete is a strong resurfacing candidate; other slabs have movement, settlement or structural problems that make replacement the better investment. The right answer begins with an honest assessment.

  • Surface scaling and worn texture
  • Staining or uneven color
  • Outdated appearance
  • Minor cracks needing preparation
  • Structurally sound underlying slab

Resurface or replace?

Make the decision based on the slab—not assumptions.

Resurfacing is best suited to structurally sound concrete with surface-level wear. Significant settlement, heaving, severe movement or a failing base may require replacement.

Good resurfacing signs
  • Cosmetic wear
  • Surface scaling
  • Discoloration
  • Outdated finish
  • Stable slab
Possible replacement signs
  • Major settlement
  • Moving structural cracks
  • Severe heaving
  • Drainage failure
  • Broken displaced sections
