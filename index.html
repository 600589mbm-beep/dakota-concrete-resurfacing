Resurface or replace?

Start with the condition of the slab—not assumptions.

Resurfacing can be an excellent fit for cosmetically worn or moderately damaged concrete. Major settlement, widespread structural failure, or severe movement may call for replacement.

✓

Often a resurfacing candidate

  • Surface scaling or worn finish
  • Staining and discoloration
  • Outdated texture or appearance
  • Minor cracks needing preparation
  • Structurally sound existing slab
!

May require replacement

  • Significant settlement or heaving
  • Large moving structural cracks
  • Severely deteriorated base
  • Drainage or grade failures
  • Extensive broken or displaced sections
